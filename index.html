Srinivasa Ramanujan Aiyangar was an Indian mathematician who lived during British Rule in India.
Despite the absence of formal education, he made significant contributions to the field of mathematics.

Here's a timeline of S. Ramanujan's life:

  • 1887 - Born in Erode, Mysore State, British Indian Empire
  • 1889 - Contracts smallpox, but recovers
  • 1892 - Enrolls at the local school
  • 1897 - Passes his primary examinations in English, Tamil, geography and arithmetic with the best scores in the district
  • 1903 - Obtains a copy of A Synopsis of Elementary Results in Pure and Applied Mathematics by G. S. Carr
  • 1904 - Graduates from Town Higher Secondary School with the K. Ranganatha Rao Prize. Receives a scholarship to study at Government Arts College
  • 1904 - Loses the scholarship because of failing all subjects except mathematics
  • 1905 - Runs away from home. Enrolls at Pachaiyappa's College in Madras
  • 1906 - Leaves college again without an FA degree
  • 1909 - Marries a girl named Janaki who was 10 years old (duh!)
  • 1910 - Begins to get recognition in Madras's mathematical circles, which leads to his inclusion as a researcher at the University of Madras
  • 1913 - Writes G. H. Hardy, a famous British mathematician
  • 1914 - Travels to England by ship, leaving his wife to stay with his parents in India
  • 1916 - Awarded a Bachelor of Arts by Research degree (the predecessor of the PhD degree) for his work on highly composite numbers
  • 1917 - Elected to the London Mathematical Society
  • 1918 - Elected a Fellow of the Royal Society, the second Indian admitted. Becomes the first Indian to be elected a Fellow of Trinity College, Cambridge
  • 1919 - Returns to Kumbakonam, Madras Presidency
  • 1920 - Dies at the age of 32 from tuberculosis (possibly from hepatic amoebiasis)
